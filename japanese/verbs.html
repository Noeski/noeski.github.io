Verb group

Group 1 う-verbs (godan). Conjugate by changing the final う-row sound. E.g. 書く → 書きます
Group 2 る-verbs (ichidan). Drop る, add ending. E.g. 食べる → 食べます
Group 3 Irregular. Only する and くる (and their compounds).

Transitivity

transitive Takes a direct object marked with を. The subject acts on something.
intransitive No direct object. The subject itself moves or changes state.

Key particles

を Direct object — what is acted upon
に Target, destination, time, or indirect object
で Location of action, means, or instrument
へ Direction (interchangeable with に for movement)
から From (origin or starting point)
と Together with / quotation marker

Usage notes

いる vs ある

Both mean "to exist / to be". いる is for living things (people, animals). ある is for inanimate objects and abstract things. Both take に for location.

行く・来る・帰る

These three motion verbs pair with に or へ for destination. 行く moves away from the speaker, 来る moves toward the speaker, 帰る means to return to a home base.

開ける vs 開く

開ける (akeru) is transitive — you open something (を). 開く (aku) is intransitive — something opens on its own. This transitive/intransitive pairing is very common in Japanese.

着る・はく・かぶる

Japanese uses different "wear" verbs depending on body part. 着る = upper body clothing. はく = lower body / feet. かぶる = head. All take を.

わかる vs しる

わかる (to understand) takes が, not を. しる (to come to know) takes を. For current knowledge state, use 知っている (not just 知る).

する — the workhorse

する can attach to many nouns to make compound verbs: 勉強する, 電話する, 結婚する, etc. It is always Group 3 (irregular) and always transitive when used with を.
