Usage notes

なかなか

Flips meaning with polarity. Positive sentence → "quite / rather good". Negative sentence → "just can't seem to". Both are very common.

やはり / やっぱり

Same word, different register. やっぱり is casual speech; やはり is more formal or written. Meaning: "as expected / after all".

あまり & 全然

Both require a negative verb to complete their meaning. You will almost never see either in a positive sentence at this level.

どんどん vs だんだん

Both express gradual change, but どんどん feels fast and energetic (things piling up / speeding up), while だんだん is a slow, smooth progression.

ずっと

Can mean duration ("all along / the whole time") or distance ("far away"). Context usually makes it clear.

もう vs まだ

もう = "already" (positive) or "not anymore" (negative). まだ = "still" (positive) or "not yet" (negative). A common pairing to master early.
